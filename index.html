Driving AI transformation at SAP.

I'm Head of Innovation Programs at SAP Labs India's Managing Director's Office. The mandate is direct: drive AI transformation at SAP. We do that through programs, initiatives, and events — enabling, scaling, and embedding AI across our engineering organization. Two outcomes we're after: amplify how engineers use AI in their day-to-day work, and bring AI into the SAP products we ship.

The job is leadership, but I'm a developer at heart. I write code with AI every day — Claude Code, Copilot, MCP servers — and I learn the development concepts as they evolve. That daily practice keeps me current with the agentic-coding craft, and it's why my teaching lands: the loops, harnesses, and prompting techniques I share aren't theory, they're what I just used to ship something.

Fifteen years in software underneath all of this — started as a Developer at TechHub (2011), then ABAP at ERP Corp, joined SAP in 2012 and have been here since. ABAP and WebDynpro to start, then Java and full-stack on SAP UI5/Node, five years as Cloud Architect on SAP BTP, Product Owner along the way. So when I talk AI transformation, it's grounded in how real software actually gets built at scale.

Beyond the day job: pursuing AI & Business Statistics at IIM Bangalore, and writing A Letter from AI — a weekly newsletter on AI-assisted software development, read by 650+ engineers and leaders.

#01 · now · the continuous practice

What I'm doing every week.

Not a list of events — a list of ways of working. The craft I practice, teach, and write about, on repeat.

the craft · core

The Art of Working with AI

The thing most people miss: AI is a collaborator, not a search bar. I teach how to talk to it, frame intent, give it context, recover from bad turns, and steer it toward the answer you actually need. Prompting is a discipline — and it's the multiplier behind every other AI workflow that works.

650+readers learning along · A Letter from AI
discipline

Loop Engineering

Designing the cycle the AI runs inside: plan → act → verify → reflect → iterate. Where to checkpoint, when to branch, how to detect drift, and how to keep humans usefully in the loop.

discipline

Harness Engineering

The scaffolding around the model — tools, MCP servers, hooks, permissions, sub-agents. A well-built harness is the difference between an AI that talks and an AI that ships.

daily stack

Claude · Copilot · MCP

Where the practice meets the keyboard. Claude Code and GitHub Copilot are my daily drivers; MCP servers wire them into real engineering systems. I learn here, then I teach what works.
